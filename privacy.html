Privacy Notice.

Effective May 2, 2026

UrClaw is a practice of Anchovy Labs LLC, operating from New York City. This notice explains what information we collect when you contact us, how we use it, who else processes it on our behalf, and the rights you have under the GDPR (if you are in the EU or UK) and the CCPA/CPRA (if you are a California resident). The controller is Anchovy Labs LLC, c/o info@urclaw.com.

What we collect

We collect only what you send us, and only what we need to respond. The categories below match the CCPA/CPRA categories of personal information.

IdentifiersName, email address, role, firm. Source: you, when you write to us or book a call.
Commercial informationEngagement memorandum, invoices, payment records. Source: you and our payment processor.
Communications contentEmail correspondence, workflow descriptions you send for the 48-hour diagram, attachments. Source: you.
Calendar metadataBooked discovery-call slot, time zone, any notes you add. Source: Cal.com on your booking.
Server logsIP address, user agent, request path, retained by GitHub Pages and Cloudflare for serving and abuse prevention. Source: your browser. Not under our control.

We do not collect sensitive personal information as defined by CPRA (no biometric, geolocation, financial account, health, or precise location data). We do not run analytics, advertising trackers, or session-recording tools on this site. We do not set first-party cookies. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

Who else processes it

We use a small set of subprocessors. We do not authorize them to use your information for their own purposes, and we will give 30 days' notice before adding a new one to engagements under DPA.

Cal.comDiscovery-call scheduling. Their notice: cal.com/privacy.
Google WorkspaceEmail and document storage for info@urclaw.com correspondence.
GitHub PagesStatic hosting of urclaw.com. Receives request logs.
CloudflareDNS and edge caching. Receives request logs and may set storage incidental to serving.

How we use it

  • Reply to your message.
  • Prepare and deliver work in your engagement memorandum.
  • Meet legal obligations (tax records, retention rules, audit requests).

Lawful basis under GDPR: your consent when you write to us; performance of contract once an engagement memorandum is signed; legitimate interests for fraud prevention, security, and recordkeeping. We do not use your information for automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effects.

How long we keep it

Prospect correspondence24 months from last contact, then deleted.
Active engagement recordsLife of the engagement plus six years (NY contract limitations period under CPLR § 213).
Tax recordsSeven years (IRS retention, 26 U.S.C. § 6501).
Server logsPer the retention policy of GitHub Pages and Cloudflare, typically 30 to 90 days.

You may ask us to delete material that is not subject to a legal hold or one of the retention rules above.

Your rights

If you are in the EU, the UK, or California, you have the rights below. To exercise any of them, email info@urclaw.com with the subject line "privacy request." We respond within 30 days (extendable once by 60 days with notice). We may need to verify your identity using information you have already shared with us.

  • Access: a copy of what we hold about you.
  • Rectification: correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: deletion of data we no longer need to keep.
  • Restriction: pause processing while a question is resolved.
  • Portability: a machine-readable export.
  • Objection: stop processing based on legitimate interests, including marketing.
  • Withdraw consent: at any time, without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • California residents: limit use of sensitive personal information (we collect none), opt out of sale or sharing (we do neither), and non-discrimination for exercising any right.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (EU/UK) or the California Privacy Protection Agency.

Security

Email is encrypted in transit between major providers. Our laptops use full-disk encryption and require multi-factor authentication on Google Workspace. We do not store credentials on the homepage. Engagement-specific security measures, including audit-trail format and access controls, are named in your engagement memorandum.

Breach notification

If we confirm a personal-data breach affecting you, we will notify you within 72 hours of confirmation, in line with GDPR Article 33. The notice will name what was affected, what we know about cause and scope, and what we are doing about it.

On-premise installs

For engagements that include on-premise deployment, we never receive a copy of the data the system processes. The agent runs on your hardware, in accounts you control. The data residency and retention rules of your installation apply to that data, not this notice.

International transfers

UrClaw operates from the United States. If you are in the EU or UK and write to us, your message crosses to a US recipient under your consent. Where we work with EU or UK personal data under an engagement memorandum, the parties execute Standard Contractual Clauses or, for UK transfers, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, named in that memorandum.

Children

This site is not directed to anyone under 18, and we do not knowingly collect information from minors. If you believe we have collected data from someone under 13, email us and we will delete it.

Changes to this notice

We may update this notice. The effective date at the top reflects the latest version. If a change materially expands what we collect or how we use it, we will email anyone in active correspondence before that change takes effect.
